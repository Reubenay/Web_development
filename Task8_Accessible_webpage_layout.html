Code your way Off Shege

“Shege” is Nigerian slang rooted in Hausa, now widely used to describe intense suffering, misfortune, or tribulation. Think of it as the upgraded version of “sapa” (being broke)—when life hits you with fuel scarcity, cash shortage, and network wahala all at once. So when someone says “code your way off shege,” they’re basically saying: “Use your tech skills—especially coding—to escape hardship and build a better life.”
